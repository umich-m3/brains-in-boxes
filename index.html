Introduction to the Neuroanatomy Small Groups

Welcome to the small group sessions!!

We encourage you to bring your laptops to class as there are a limited number of modules. We will provide additional brains and brain sections to supplement the use of "virtual" modules.

At this point in your medical career you are aware that every human body is unique, that none fits the standard description in all respects. The human brain is no exception. In the Neuroanatomy Laboratory, you have the opportunity to examine a variety of plastinated brain specimens, each of which may be understood with reference to the standard, but none of which is an exact match to the standard.

The eleven laboratory modules and supplemental material that follow will be your guide. Each module includes "standard" examples of each region of the brain. In each laboratory session you will study three or four assigned modules and supplementary material. Each module contains a printed guide (a PowerPoint presentation on that topic) and necessary anatomical specimens and models required to learn and interpret the standard pattern on the individual samples. The printed guide and quizzes are available on-line. You are expected to review this material prior to coming to Neurolab (Prelab). Feel free to print the material or, better yet, bring your laptop computer to the laboratory.

In the laboratory you will learn through three-dimensional visual exploration and the sensorimotor manipulation of the plastinated brain specimens and models. Memorizing the appearance of the pictures in the PowerPoint presentations is not a substitute for this exploration. Supplemental material, some of which you have seen in the Gross Anatomy laboratory, is also presented. A glossary of terms and additional 3-D models will be available. We would appreciate your feed-back concerning the usefulness of these materials.

The final examination for this sequence will include questions on the material presented in the eleven modules as part of the practical exam.
